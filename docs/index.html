What Is Wikispeedia?

Wikipedia is a beast. A beast which is also many people’s best friend. Haven’t we all been in a situation where we just had to know the name of Napoleon's mother? And her dogs? In this case, the link structure of Wikipedia can be quite helpful. Going into the page of Napoleon you might very well find the name of his mother, and a link to the Wikipedia page of her life where you find her dog’s name. This is the logic which should determine the link structure of Wikipedia. On each page, links should be available to other pages which are related to the first page.

Wikispeedia

This intuition is what the game Wikispeedia builds on. The game challenges the user to go from page A to page B only by using the links of the respective pages. Stanford University collected data on ~76000 of these games, recording users' navigation through a subset consisting of ~4600 pages of Wikipedia 2009. This allows us to study how the subset is connected and how users chose to navigate these paths.

What do the paths that you take look like? How do they differ from the shortest path between the two articles?

Exploratory Data Analysis

Collaborative authorship is the key aspect to what has allowed Wikipedia to grow into the beast that it is. Wikipedia has around 44,8 million registered users who can contribute and as of the 19th December 2022, more than 120 000 users made updates during the preceding 30 days. This results in an evolutionary process of each page, and of each page’s links. Therefore, the structure of the network made up of links and pages will change.

What does this change look like from 2009 to 2022?
