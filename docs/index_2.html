What if links are clicked just because they appear on the top?

On the previous page, we introduced a few factors that can be used to characterise the importance of a page. Now we propose a new, simpler factor:

What if Wikispeedia players just click the first link they see?

Link Position

This graph shows the position on the page of links. The distribution of the shortest-path link is roughly uniform, but the distribution of the links that the players actually clicked on is heavily skewed towards the top of the page.

So we're done! ... right?

Unfortunately, no. There are many confounding factors that can affect the probability of a player clicking a link, and we need to account for them in order to isolate the effect of link position. In the previous page, we identified Pagerank and Category as two factors that greatly affect the link structure of Wikispeedia, but on top of these, there are many other confounding factors, like human intuition, that we cannot manually account for.

We need to carefully choose a control in order to account for these confounders. Luckily, we do have a predictor for semantic similarity: Graph embeddings! Given a page and a target destination, we can calculate the page that is closest to the target destination in the embedding space. This page can serve as a control pair to the link that the player actually clicked on.

The shortest path links is uniformly distributed over the pages. However, users are biased towards links that appear higher on the page.
